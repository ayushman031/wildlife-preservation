Our Mission

To protect and preserve India's rich wildlife heritage for future generations through conservation, education, and community engagement.

Wildlife Conservation India works tirelessly to safeguard endangered species, restore critical habitats, and promote harmonious coexistence between humans and wildlife. Our holistic approach combines scientific research, on-ground conservation efforts, and community-based initiatives to create lasting impact across the nation.

Species Protection

Safeguarding endangered wildlife through anti-poaching and habitat protection

Habitat Restoration

Rebuilding natural ecosystems in India's biodiversity hotspots

Community Empowerment

Engaging local communities in conservation efforts

Our Vision

A thriving India where wildlife and humans coexist in harmony, with restored ecosystems and protected biodiversity.

We envision an India where every citizen values and actively participates in wildlife conservation. Our goal is to create a sustainable future where endangered species recover, natural habitats flourish, and communities benefit from healthy ecosystems.

Our Story

1995 - Foundation

Wildlife Conservation India was founded by a group of passionate conservationists in response to declining tiger populations across the country.

2000 - First Major Success

Established the first community-managed wildlife corridor in the Western Ghats, connecting fragmented tiger habitats.

2010 - National Recognition

Received the prestigious Wildlife Conservation Award from the Government of India for our tiger conservation efforts.

2015 - Expansion

Expanded operations to cover 15 states, working across 100+ wildlife sanctuaries and national parks.

2024 - Present Day

Now protecting 50+ endangered species with 25,000+ volunteers and partners across India, making a real difference every day.
